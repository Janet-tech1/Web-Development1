## Sticky Positioning Document

Sticky Positioning Demo

Sticky Header - Scroll down and watch me stick!

Section 1

This is the first section of content. Notice how the header above stays "sticky" at the top when you scroll down through this container.

Sticky positioning is perfect for navigation bars, table headers, and section dividers.

Section 2

Here's more content to scroll through. The sticky header should remain visible at the top of the scrollable container.

Unlike fixed positioning, sticky elements stay within their parent container.

Section 3

Keep scrolling to see the sticky behavior in action. This is commonly used in modern web design for improved user experience.

Sticky positioning requires a threshold value (top, bottom, left, or right) to work properly.

Section 4

Final section of content. The sticky header will disappear when you reach the end of its containing element.

This makes sticky positioning perfect for section-based layouts and long-form content.
